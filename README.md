# ECC3479 Project

## Research Question

How are changes in international student enrolments associated with rental vacancy rates in Melbourne?

This project examines whether international student demand is descriptively associated with Melbourne rental vacancy rates. The analysis is exploratory and descriptive, not causal.

## Data Sources

### International student data

International student data are obtained from the Australian Government Department of Education international student monthly summary and pivot data.

Source:  
https://www.education.gov.au/international-education-data-and-research/international-student-monthly-summary-and-data-tables

The dataset uses Victoria year-to-date international student enrolments and commencements at quarterly reference points:

- Q1 = March
- Q2 = June
- Q3 = September
- Q4 = December

### Rental vacancy data

Melbourne rental vacancy data are based on public SQM Research vacancy charts.

Source:  
https://sqmresearch.com.au/

The underlying SQM data table requires purchase, so the Melbourne vacancy-rate values used in this project are visually approximated from the public chart. This limitation is documented in `data/raw/README.md`.

## Primary Econometric Analysis

The primary econometric analysis examines the descriptive relationship between Victorian international student enrolments and the estimated Melbourne rental vacancy rate.

The primary result is that the estimated relationship between international student enrolments and the Melbourne rental vacancy rate is negative but statistically insignificant. This means that the available quarterly data do not provide strong statistical evidence of a stable negative relationship.

The analysis is descriptive rather than causal. It does not claim that changes in international student enrolments causally affect rental vacancy rates.

## Robustness Analysis

The robustness analysis is contained in:

```text
code/robustness_analysis.ipynb
```

The analysis restates the main descriptive result from the primary econometric analysis and tests whether it is sensitive to alternative specifications.

The robustness checks include:

- no controls
- adding a linear time trend
- adding international student commencements as an additional control
- restricting the sample to 2022–2025
- dropping outliers using the 5th–95th percentile rule
- using log enrolments instead of enrolments in levels
- using HC3 robust standard errors instead of HC1

The robustness results suggest that the main negative and statistically insignificant relationship is fragile rather than robust. Some alternative specifications produce positive and statistically significant estimates. Therefore, the final interpretation remains descriptive rather than causal.

## EDA Findings

The exploratory data analysis compares Victoria international student enrolments with estimated Melbourne vacancy rates from 2020 Q1 to 2025 Q4.

The time-series patterns show that international student enrolments changed substantially during and after the COVID-affected period. The estimated Melbourne vacancy rate was relatively high around 2020–2021 and then declined in later years.

However, the scatter plot and correlation analysis do not show a clear contemporaneous linear relationship between Victorian international student enrolments and Melbourne vacancy rates. These results should be interpreted cautiously because:

- the international student data are measured at the Victoria level
- the vacancy data refer to Melbourne
- the vacancy-rate values are approximated from SQM public charts
- the 2020–2025 period is strongly affected by COVID-era disruptions

Therefore, the current analysis is descriptive and exploratory rather than causal.
