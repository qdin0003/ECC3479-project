## Quarterly data update

The project now includes an updated quarterly dataset for 2020 Q1 to 2025 Q4. This update uses a more detailed Department of Education international student pivot table and an approximated Melbourne vacancy series based on SQM public charts.

Compared with the original annual dataset, the new quarterly dataset provides more time variation and separates international student enrolments from commencements. This is useful because commencements may better reflect new student inflows, while enrolments capture the broader stock of international student activity.

However, the dataset has two important limitations. First, the international student data are measured at the Victoria level, while the vacancy data refer to Melbourne. Second, the Melbourne vacancy values are approximated from public SQM charts rather than obtained from the purchased underlying SQM table. For this reason, the updated dataset is used for descriptive exploratory analysis rather than causal estimation.

The main expected relationship is negative: when international student enrolments increase, rental vacancy rates may fall because stronger student demand can reduce available rental supply. However, this relationship may be affected by COVID-era restrictions, rental supply changes, migration patterns, interest rates and other housing-market factors.
